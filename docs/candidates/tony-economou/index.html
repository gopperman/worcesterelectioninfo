Tony Economou

Candidate, D1

Who is Tony Economou?

Tony Economou served as Worcester's District 1 Councilor from 2011-2017. Economou has worked in the real-estate industry for over 26 years, and represents the interests of real estate developers and landlords alike. His top priority for the city is to "bring back common sense", a dog whistle for folks who would like to silence activists seeking more action from the city council on police misconduct, cooperation with ICE, inhumane policies towards the homeless, and rising LGBTQ+ hate.

What are Tony Economou's Political Positions?

  • Housing: Economou opposes several initiatives to make housing affordable, including rent stabilization and a small fee on high-end real estate transactions to create an affordable housing fund. When asked what policies support to keep Worcester affordable, he gave no answer. source
  • Education: Economou wants to build a new Burncoat High School and opposes the expansion of charter schools. source
  • Public Trandsportation: Economou opposes fare-free buses, a wildly-popular transportation program that provides 3-4 million free trips annually, and is supported by 70% of Worcester residents. He also opposes the expansion of protected bike lanes in the city, including the Mill Street redesign that has reduced automotive speeding and crashes. source
  • Immigration: When asked "What can your city do to help protect immigrant communities under ICE and ensure genuine community safe?", Economou simply answered, "The community is safe". source
  • Sustainability: Economou opposes adopting building codes that would mandate new construction and renovations make use of modern, high-efficiency heating and cooling systems. source
